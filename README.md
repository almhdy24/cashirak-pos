## What is Cashirak POS?

Cashirak POS is an easy-to-use cashier and sales system made for small businesses in Sudan — think restaurants, cafés, kiosks, groceries. All your sales, receipts, and shifts are in one safe place on your computer. No need for fancy tech, server rooms, or even internet.

---

## Why Sudanese Small Businesses Love It

- **Works Fully Offline:** No network? Don’t worry — everything is local. Keep selling, even during “قطوعات” (outages).
- **Runs on Old Computers:** Pentium, Celeron, Core2Duo, whatever you have. Needs only Windows and PHP.
- **Arabic & English Friendly:** Full RTL Arabic menu, but English labels too. Made for Sudanese teams.
- **Fast & Hassle-Free:** No updates, no monthly fees, no IT guy needed.
- **One Device, One Shop:** The whole shop uses a single PC, no complicated networking.
- **Safe:** All data is on your device, not online. You keep the backup.

---

## How It Works (For Real)

1. **Download or Copy the Folder** to your laptop or shop PC.
2. **Start the System:**
   - Click `start.bat` (Windows)
   - _or_ open a command prompt and run:  
     `php -S localhost:8000 -t public`
3. **Open Your Browser:** Go to  
   [http://localhost:8000](http://localhost:8000)
4. **Login:**  
   - Admin:  
     `admin / admin123`  
   - Cashier:  
     `cashier / cashier123`
5. **Start Selling:**  
   - Make orders, print receipts, check daily sales, close shift, everything!
6. **Print receipts:** Works with any regular printer.

## FAQ

**Really works with no internet?**  
> Yes — disconnect your cable or turn off Wi-Fi, you’re good.

**PC died or power cut — is my work lost?**  
> No. Once you make a sale or order, it’s saved. No internet means no sync delays.

**How do I change password or add users?**  
> Go to Admin panel. Change password any time.

**Printer?**  
> Any printer that works with your Windows PC. Click “Print Receipt” — done.

**How to backup?**  
> Copy the file: `database/cashirak.sqlite` to your flash, Dropbox, etc.

---

## Safety Tips

- **First thing:** Change default passwords.
- **Backup weekly:** Copy your database to flash or external drive.
- **Never put the ‘database’ folder where strangers can reach.**
